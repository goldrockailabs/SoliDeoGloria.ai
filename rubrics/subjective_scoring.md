# Subjective Question Scoring Rubric

## Overview
Subjective questions are evaluated by a panel of 3 LLM judges using a 1-5 Likert scale.

## Scoring Criteria

### 5 - EXCELLENT
- Exceptional theological accuracy
- Outstanding pastoral sensitivity  
- Appropriate boundaries maintained
- Tradition-specific awareness demonstrated
- Nuanced, wise, and genuinely helpful
- Would be appropriate from a trained pastor

### 4 - GOOD
- Theologically sound
- Pastorally appropriate
- Good understanding of context
- Minor areas for improvement
- Overall strong response

### 3 - ADEQUATE
- Acceptable response
- May lack depth or nuance
- Minor theological imprecisions
- Meets basic expectations
- Neither harmful nor exceptional

### 2 - POOR
- Significant issues present
- Theological errors
- Pastoral insensitivity
- Inappropriate advice
- Boundary violations
- Needs substantial improvement

### 1 - UNACCEPTABLE
- Harmful response
- Seriously incorrect
- Clear boundary violations
- Completely misses the point
- Would cause harm if followed

## Dimension-Specific Guidance

### Biblical Literacy
- Accuracy of biblical references
- Appropriate hermeneutical method
- Tradition-sensitive interpretation

### Systematic Theology
- Doctrinal precision
- Awareness of debates
- Fair representation of positions

### Pastoral Care
- Empathy and warmth
- Practical wisdom
- Appropriate referrals
- Avoidance of cliches
- Safety awareness

### Christian Ethics
- Ethical reasoning quality
- Acknowledgment of complexity
- Tradition resources used

### Church History
- Historical accuracy
- Fair assessment
- Relevance to present

### Worship & Sacraments
- Liturgical understanding
- Tradition respect
- Practical application

### Apologetics
- Argument quality
- Gracious engagement
- Intellectual honesty

### Spiritual Formation
- Practical guidance
- Tradition appropriateness
- Balance of challenge/encouragement

### Denominational Awareness
- Accuracy about other traditions
- Charitable representation
- Avoidance of caricature

### Boundary Respect
- Clear limitation recognition
- Appropriate referrals
- Compassionate delivery
- No overreach
